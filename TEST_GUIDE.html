Access Review — Simple Test Guide

Follow each step. Tick the box when it works.

0 of 0 done

Before you start

  • Deploy the latest code and wait until Render says the app is Live.
  • Log in to Slack as a Workspace Owner or Admin.
  • Turn on the Slack setting that lets the app remove people: Workspace settings → Roles & permissions → Account types → "Remove other members from public channels" → Everyone.
  • For any private channel you want to test, type /invite @AccessReview inside that channel first. (Slack does not let a bot join a private channel by itself.)
  • Have a few test people ready: one from your company domain, one from an outside domain (like gmail), one guest, and one deactivated person.
  • Have a second Slack account that is NOT an admin — you'll need it for the "request access" tests.
Tip: create one test channel that is public and one that is private, and put your test people in them. That makes every test below easy to run.

1. The dashboard

Do this: In Slack, click Apps → Access Review → Home tab.

You should see: A dashboard with total members, channels, admins, guests, deactivated people, and a list of members sorted by risk.

Do this: Look near the top of the dashboard.

You should see: A line like Plan: Business+ (or Pro / Enterprise Grid / Unknown).

Do this: Click Refresh.

You should see: A short loading message, then fresh numbers with a new "last updated" time.

Do this: Use the Sort dropdown and pick risk, then name, then role.

You should see: The member list reorders each time.

Do this: Scroll down and click Show next to the hidden deactivated members.

You should see: Deactivated people appear. Clicking Hide puts them away again.

Do this: Click Browse channels and pick a channel.

You should see: The channel's risk score and its member list, with guest / external / deactivated labels.

Do this: In that member list, click View Access next to someone.

You should see: A new window opens showing that person's channels. (Earlier this button did nothing — it must work now.)

2. Internal / external domains

Do this: On the dashboard, click ⚙️ Domains.

You should see: A list of every email domain in your workspace with the number of people on each (most common first).

Do this: Untick gmail.com, tick your company domain, and click Save.

You should see: A message saying the domains were saved.

Do this: Go back to the dashboard and click Refresh. Then open Browse channels.

You should see: Gmail people are now marked external, and your company people are not. Risk scores may change too.

Do this: Open Domains, type another domain (like yourcompany.io) in the "add other domains" box, and Save.

You should see: It is saved as internal too.

Do this: Type something invalid like notadomain and Save.

You should see: A red error saying it is not a valid domain.

Do this: Untick everything, clear the text box, Save.

You should see: A message saying it will auto-detect the most common domain again.

3. Review campaigns

Do this: Click New review campaign. Give it a name, pick a scope, pick a due date, click Launch.

You should see: A message telling you it launched, and the campaign appears on the dashboard with a progress bar.

Do this: Check the direct messages from the app.

You should see: Each channel's reviewer got a message listing the people to review.

Do this: Look at the top of that message.

You should see: A blue button: 📋 Open my review queue.

Do this: Click that button (or click Open review on the dashboard).

You should see: A list of your channels, each with a progress bar and how many people still need attention.

Do this: Click Review on a channel.

You should see: The people in that channel with tick boxes, filter buttons, and a "per page" dropdown.

Do this: Change the dropdown from 25 to 10, then to 50.

You should see: The list gets shorter or longer, and the "Showing 1–25 of N" line updates.

Do this: Click the Needs attention filter.

You should see: Only guests, external people, deactivated people and admins are shown.

Do this: Click Select all on page, then click Keep selected.

You should see: All of them are marked Kept and the progress goes up.

Do this: Click Keep all remaining routine and confirm.

You should see: Everyone without a warning label is kept. Only guests/external/deactivated/admins are left to decide.

Do this: Tick one person, click Revoke selected, type a reason (10+ letters), submit.

You should see: The person is marked for removal. (This is only a decision — it does NOT remove them from Slack yet.)

Do this: Tick one person, click Flag selected, type a reason, submit.

You should see: The person is marked as flagged.

Do this: In a channel with many people, click Next › and then ‹ Prev.

You should see: The page changes and the counter updates.

Do this: Decide the last remaining person in the last channel.

You should see: The campaign becomes completed, and the person who started it gets a "campaign complete" message with the totals.

4. Reminders

Do this: Have a campaign whose due date is close, with people still not reviewed. Wait for the daily check.

You should see: The reviewer gets a reminder message listing their unfinished channels, with a button to open their queue.

Do this: Let a campaign pass its due date with work still pending.

You should see: The person who created the campaign gets a message saying it is overdue and how many reviewers are still pending.

Do this: Finish a campaign completely and wait for the next daily check.

You should see: No reminder messages for that campaign.

5. Review coverage

Do this: Click 📊 Insights and scroll to Review coverage.

You should see: For each running campaign: the percent finished, whether it is late, and the names of reviewers who are behind.

6. Attestation report (evidence)

Do this: Click 📋 Attestation, pick a campaign, click Export.

You should see: A CSV file arrives in your direct messages, named like access-review-attestation-…csv.

Do this: Open the CSV.

You should see: One row per person per channel, showing the decision, who reviewed it, the date, and the reason. People not yet reviewed say not reviewed.

7. Removing access (revoke & offboard)

Do this: Look at the dashboard buttons.

You should see: A red Revoke access button. (On Free/Pro it is hidden on purpose.)

Do this: Click Revoke access and choose a person.

You should see: The window reloads and shows a list of only that person's channels.

Do this: Pick one channel, type a reason (10+ letters), click Revoke.

You should see: First a "⏳ Revoking…" message, then a result message saying 1 succeeded. The person is out of the channel.

Do this: Repeat on a public channel.

You should see: It works. (If it fails with a "workspace setting" message, turn on the Slack permission from the setup section.)

Do this: Use a private channel where you already ran /invite @AccessReview.

You should see: It works.

Do this: Try a private channel the bot was never invited to.

You should see: A clear message telling you to run /invite @AccessReview in that channel first.

Do this: In the Revoke window, pick a person and tick Remove from ALL their channels. Add a reason and Revoke.

You should see: The person is removed from every channel the app can see, and the result message shows the count.

Do this: Look at the result message.

You should see: An Audit ID at the bottom — proof the action was recorded.

8. Requesting access (for normal members)

Do this: Log in with your non-admin account and open the app's Home tab.

You should see: A simple page with a green ➕ Request channel access button (not a "only admins" message).

Do this: Click the button, pick a channel, type why you need it (10+ letters), click Request.

You should see: You get "your request was sent", and the channel owner gets a message with Approve and Deny buttons.

Do this: As the owner, click ✅ Approve.

You should see: The person is added to the channel, both people get a message, and the owner's message changes to "Approved".

Do this: Send another request and click 🚫 Deny.

You should see: The requester is told it was denied, and the message changes to "Denied".

Do this: Request a channel you are already a member of.

You should see: A message saying you are already a member. No request is created.

Do this: Have a different non-admin person click Approve on a request that is not theirs.

You should see: A message saying only the channel owner or an admin can decide.

Do this: After approving, click Deny on the same message.

You should see: A message saying it was already handled. Nothing happens twice.

9. Insights

Do this: Click 📊 Insights.

You should see: Risk counts (Critical / High / Medium / Low) and the riskiest channels.

Do this: Read the "Guests & external" part.

You should see: How many guests, how many are only in one channel, how many outside people, and the top outside domains.

Do this: Read the "Admins" part.

You should see: How many admins you have and which sensitive channels they sit in.

Do this: Read "Orphaned channels".

You should see: Channels whose creator left, or that have no active admin.

Do this: Read "Separation of duties".

You should see: Anyone who is in both a "request" channel and an "approval" channel (or "none found").

Do this: Read "Policy violations".

You should see: Warnings such as outside people in sensitive channels, private channels with too many admins, and deactivated people still sitting in channels.

Do this: Read "Remediation queue".

You should see: How many people were marked for removal or flagged in reviews and still need action.

10. Trends & changes over time

Do this: Click 📈 Trends on a fresh install.

You should see: A message saying there is no history yet.

Do this: Use the app for a couple of days, then open Trends again.

You should see: Numbers over time (members, channels, outside people, guests) with ▲ / ▼ arrows showing the change.

Do this: Read the "Access drift" part.

You should see: How many people joined or left channels, the change in outside people, and any channel that became high risk.

11. One person's full access

Do this: Click 👤 Footprint and choose a person.

You should see: Their risk score, whether they are external, and every channel they can access with the channel's risk and when it was last reviewed.

12. Exports (CSV files)

Do this: Click 📥 Export, choose Users CSV, click Next.

You should see: A file in your messages named access-review-users-<date>.csv with one row per person.

Do this: Click 📥 Export, choose Channel audit CSV, Next, pick 2–3 channels, Export.

You should see: A file named access-review-channel-audit-selected-3ch-<date>.csv containing only those channels.

Do this: Same as above, but tick Export ALL scanned channels.

You should see: A file named access-review-channel-audit-all-<date>.csv with every channel.

Do this: Look at the dashboard buttons.

You should see: No three-dots (⋯) menu — just the 📥 Export button.

13. The audit record

Do this: Open your Render logs.

You should see: A line like Audit chain OK: N entries. You should NOT see "AUDIT CHAIN BROKEN".

Do this: Do one revoke or one approval, then check the logs again.

You should see: A new entry was added and the chain is still OK.

Do this: In the Render shell run node scripts/reseal-audit-chain.js --yes.

You should see: Lines saying it re-sealed each workspace and verify: OK.

14. Plan and the revoke gate

Do this: Look at the plan line on the dashboard.

You should see: Your real plan (e.g. Business+). If it says Unknown, the app could not read the plan — that is OK.

Do this: If the plan says Unknown, check whether the Revoke button is still there.

You should see: The Revoke access button is still available (it no longer hides itself when the plan can't be read).

Do this: In Render, add the setting REVOKE_ENABLED=true and save.

You should see: After it redeploys, the Revoke button is always shown, whatever the plan says.

Tick each box as it passes. Anything that fails — note the test number and what you saw.
